# London — sample itinerary paste

**Trip:** Early June 2026, 6 days, group of 4 friends in their late 30s
**Source mix:** friend texts (Sarah, Tom), Eater London, ChatGPT, r/london, TikTok, a Rick Steves audio guide chapter
**Why this represents the demographic:** Type-A trip-lead of a 4-friend group — English-speaking so the "research load" feels lower, but reservation density (theatre, St. JOHN, Hawksmoor) and day-trip coordination (Bath, Oxford, Cotswolds) actually surface the same eight phases.

---

## What got pasted in

- British Museum — free, timed entry slots, get the 10am one to do Rosetta Stone + Parthenon Marbles before tour groups
- The National Gallery — Trafalgar Square, free, 2 hrs is plenty for highlights
- Tate Modern — free permanent collection, special exhibits ticketed, the Turbine Hall is the move
- Tower of London — book online, the Beefeater Yeoman Warder tour is included and excellent
- Westminster Abbey — book online, closed to tourists Sundays (services only)
- Buckingham Palace State Rooms — summer opening only (July–September), book exact dates
- The London Eye — book Fast Track or skip it, honestly Sky Garden is free and better
- Borough Market — Fri/Sat (Mon-Thu reduced), lunch crowds peak 12–2, Bread Ahead doughnuts
- Camden Market — Sundays, food court at the lock, the rest is mostly tourist tat
- Notting Hill — Portobello Market Saturday (antiques Fri-Sat), Books for Cooks bookshop
- Shoreditch street art walking tour — Brick Lane area, Sundays for the markets too
- Sky Garden — FREE but book 3 weeks ahead, Fenchurch Street, sunset is the move
- Dishoom Covent Garden — no reservations weekends, queue OR go before 5pm OR breakfast bacon naan
- St. JOHN — Smithfield, Fergus Henderson, nose-to-tail, book ahead, the bone marrow + parsley salad
- Padella — Borough Market area, no reservations, queue 30+ min, the pici cacio e pepe
- The Wolseley — Piccadilly, afternoon tea OR breakfast (the kippers), book ahead
- Hawksmoor Seven Dials — steaks, book ahead, the Sunday roast at any branch is a separate move
- Tayyabs — Whitechapel, lamb chops, walk-in only (no reservations), BYOB
- Hyde Park — Serpentine Galleries are free, Speakers' Corner Sunday morning
- Kew Gardens — half-day, tube to Kew Gardens station, the Palm House
- Hampton Court Palace — day trip, train from Waterloo, the maze
- Day trip: Bath — 1h15 by train from Paddington, book the Roman Baths timed entry, Sally Lunn's buns
- Day trip: Oxford — 1h train from Marylebone, college walking tour, Bodleian Library tour
- Day trip: Cotswolds — Bourton-on-the-Water + Stow-on-the-Wold (rent a car or do a Mad Max Tours day)
- A West End show — Les Misérables at the Sondheim Theatre, book ahead, balcony cheap seats fine
- Shakespeare's Globe — summer outdoor performances, £5 standing "groundling" tickets
- Daunt Books Marylebone — the Edwardian travel bookshop, just go for the photos
- https://maps.app.goo.gl/4XmL2bYqRfTpKn9D8 — Sarah's Soho cocktail bar pin (she thinks it's Swift on Old Compton St, ground floor not the basement)
- ChatGPT day 5: morning at V&A (free), lunch at Comptoir Libanais in Kensington, afternoon at Natural History Museum, evening pub crawl in Belgravia
- r/london: Maltby Street Market — Saturdays only, Bermondsey, "Borough Market without the tourists, get the gin smoothie"
- From Tom: "the rooftop sushi place at the top of Heron Tower in the City — Sushi Samba? something like that, view is unreal"
- The National Gallery — already on the list (dedupe candidate)
- Possibly closed: The Magic Circle headquarters — open to public only on rare scheduled evenings, check website
