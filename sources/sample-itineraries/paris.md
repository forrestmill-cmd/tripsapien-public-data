## What got pasted in

- Eiffel Tower — book summit tickets 60 days out (sells fast); Erin said dusk for the light show
- Louvre — closed Tuesdays, book timed entry, just do Denon wing (Mona Lisa, Winged Victory) and leave
- Musée d'Orsay — closed Mondays, Impressionists 5th floor
- Sainte-Chapelle — timed entry, go morning for the stained glass
- Versailles — closed Mondays, book combo with Trianon + gardens; full day, RER C
- Arc de Triomphe rooftop — separate ticket, underpass entry (don't cross the Étoile)
- Catacombs of Paris — book skip-the-line a week ahead, regularly sells out
- Sacré-Cœur + wander Montmartre afternoon (Place du Tertre is a tourist trap, skip)
- Wander Le Marais — Jess's shopping list: Merci, Ofr., Empreintes, Sézane mothership
- Jardin du Luxembourg — picnic from the rue Mouffetard market
- Musée National Picasso (Le Marais)
- Frenchie Bar à Vins — Eater Paris top wine bar, walk-in 6:30pm, 5/6 rue du Nil
- Septime — reservation EXACTLY 21 days out at midnight Paris time, 11th
- Le Comptoir du Relais — Saint-Germain, Yves Camdeborde, dinner books open 21 days out
- Breizh Café — galettes, multiple locations, Marais original is the move
- Pierre Hermé Bonaparte — macarons (Ispahan, Mogador), closed Mondays
- Du Pain et des Idées — Canal Saint-Martin, closed weekends, escargot pistache
- Café de Flore — touristy but the morning coffee + croissant is the move (avoid lunch)
- Marché des Enfants Rouges — lunch stalls, closed Mondays
- Père Lachaise — Jim Morrison, Oscar Wilde, Édith Piaf; bring the printed map from the gate
- Berthillon — Île Saint-Louis, ice cream, closed Mon/Tue
- Le Mary Celeste — cocktails, Marais, walk-in
- Day trip: Giverny — Monet's gardens, open April through November only, train from Saint-Lazare to Vernon + shuttle
