# Rome — sample itinerary paste

**Trip:** April 2026, 6 days, 40th birthday with a group of 5
**Source mix:** friend texts (Marco, Sara, Adam), Eater Rome, ChatGPT, r/Italytravel, TikTok saves, the Lonely Planet Italy chapter
**Why this represents the demographic:** Type-A trip-lead organizing a milestone-birthday group of 5, where every reservation has to be coordinated weeks in advance — peak Vatican/Borghese/Roscioli pain.

---

## What got pasted in

- Vatican Museums + Sistine Chapel — book "Skip the Line" + early access (8am opening); 3 hrs minimum
- St. Peter's Basilica — free entry, dome climb is a separate ticket (book that too, 551 steps)
- Colosseum — book the combo ticket (Roman Forum + Palatine Hill, valid 24 hrs)
- Galleria Borghese — timed entry every 2 hrs, book EXACTLY 30 days out, 2-hr visit slot
- Pantheon — entry is now ticketed (€5), free Sundays + EU residents under 25
- Trevi Fountain — early morning (7am) or late night (after 11) to skip the crowd
- Spanish Steps — sit on them = €400 fine, just walk by
- Piazza Navona — coffee at a side café, NOT the main piazza-facing ones
- Wander Trastevere evenings — Marco's list of 4 bars: Freni e Frizioni, Bar San Calisto, Pimm's Good, Niji Roma
- Monti — Via del Boschetto for vintage shopping, aperitivo at Ai Tre Scalini
- Roscioli — Salumeria con Cucina, reservations 30+ days out, the cacio e pepe is the order
- Pizzarium Bonci — Prati, walk-in, slice pizza al taglio, near the Vatican
- Da Enzo al 29 — Trastevere, no reservations, arrive 7pm sharp or queue 90 min
- Bonci Pizzarium — same as Pizzarium Bonci above (Sara wrote it twice in her text)
- Sant'Eustachio Il Caffè — espresso near Pantheon, ask for it "senza zucchero" if you don't want it pre-sweetened
- Gelateria del Teatro — three locations, the one near Via dei Coronari is best
- Trapizzino — Trastevere and Testaccio branches, the chicken cacciatora one
- Tonnarello — Trastevere, walk-in, expect a queue, the cacio e pepe is the move
- Pierluigi — fish/seafood, Piazza de' Ricci near Campo de' Fiori, book ahead, splurge dinner
- Day trip: Tivoli — Villa d'Este (fountains) + Hadrian's Villa, full day, train from Tiburtina + bus
- Castel Sant'Angelo — book online, rooftop has the Vatican view
- Catacombs of San Callisto — closed Wednesdays, on the Appian Way
- Borghese Gardens — picnic from Eataly or a market
- https://maps.app.goo.gl/RnT8Dq2MwBfXKuyL7 — Adam's rooftop bar pin in Trastevere ("the one with the view of Gianicolo, doesn't remember the name")
- ChatGPT day 4: Aventine Keyhole at sunrise, Giardino degli Aranci, Roseto Comunale, lunch at Flavio al Velavevodetto in Testaccio, Non-Catholic Cemetery (Keats and Shelley graves)
- r/Italytravel rec: Armando al Pantheon — book 90+ days out for the carbonara, two seatings only
- From Sara: "the carbonara place near the Jewish Ghetto with the green awning — Nonna Betta? Sora Margherita? something like that"
- Eater Rome: Salumeria Roscioli (same as the Roscioli above — dedupe)
- TikTok save: I Supplì on Via di San Francesco a Ripa, the cacio e pepe supplì
- Possibly closed: Domus Aurea — open weekends only, advance booking required, sometimes shut for restoration
